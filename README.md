## Description

Our game focuses on the player taking on the role of a parking booth attendant for animals. Animals arrive at the booth and the player needs to interact with them and decide whether to let them enter. Before approving each animal, the player performs a small routine made up of different actions. Players can choose what order to complete the routine in and how many actions are chosen during this process.

As the game progresses, the order of these actions starts to matter. The animals arriving at the booth will have a shape, and the correct order of actions corresponds to that shape, although the player is not explicitly told this. Players have to notice patterns and recreate their previous actions.

The outside environment of the booth changes depending on the player’s actions. The weather reflects the emotional and mental state of the player. The weather gets worse if the player does not repeat the correct routine in the right order. This represents the anxiety and discomfort that can occur with OCD when rituals are not completed [1].

The goal of the game is to complete the shift by processing animals while managing the routine and remembering patterns in the system created by the player themselves.

## 5. Iteration Notes

### a. POST-PLAYTEST

_Observations_

Through our playtesting session, we found that players were able to correctly guess the disability and showed strong engagement through their verbal reactions and facial expressions. This suggests that the game successfully promotes understanding while remaining engaging and entertaining. Additionally, players found the UI clear and easy to understand, and it was obvious which buttons were clickable.

Players also showed confusion. They initially thought they were the animal in the car because there was no icon indicating the player’s avatar. It was also difficult for the player to realize that different shapes required different action sequences as there were no instructions at the start of the game. Players were also confused about when the game would end and how the difficulty would increase. Additionally, because the functions are arranged from left to right, players tended to instinctively choose them in that order for each car.

_Feedback_

Several improvements were suggested to enhance gameplay. These suggestions included making the game more challenging by introducing preselected items to guide player actions, allowing multiple cars to appear simultaneously, and adding more noticeable environmental distractions, such as weather effects that blur the screen.

For clarity, it was suggested to add a time limit so players know when the round is about to end. Participants also recommended including a counter to track correct and incorrect actions so players can better monitor their progress. Additional suggestions were to add instructions and player icons to help players better understand the mechanics and their role in the game world.

_3 Concrete Changes_

Based on our feedback and observations, we decided to add a button that allows players to read the game instructions (1) so they are not immediately thrown into the gameplay. We also added a player icon (2) to clearly indicate the player’s role. Lastly, we chose to implement a screen-blurring weather effect (3) to make the consequences more severe and noticeable.

---

### b. POST-SHOWCASE

_Two planned improvements_

- Adding more challenge/time element: morning shift (1 min), afternoon shift (20 seconds), night shift (10 secs) (infinite mode)
- Increasing the complexity of the rituals (e.g. how many times pen is clicked, where stamp is located…)

_First Improvement_

After the final showcase, our team identified opportunities to increase the challenge and replayability of the game. Players were able to understand the mechanics relatively quickly, so we proposed adding different difficulty phases such as morning, afternoon, and night shifts, with a potential infinite mode to extend gameplay and maintain engagement.
An example can be, in morning shifts the player has a set time (1 minutes) compared to night shifts where the times are shorter (10 seconds). This also acts as a factor that makes the levels more challenging as the day progresses.

_Second Improvement_

We also suggested making the rituals more demanding by increasing precision requirements, such as varying how many times the pen must be clicked or changing the location of the stamp. This supports the overall game idea better, by providing a more accurate and immersive experience.
These are the two implementations we came up with, these adjustments are necessary to create a more dynamic and challenging experience while reinforcing the repetitive and detail-focused mechanics that reflect the theme of the game.
